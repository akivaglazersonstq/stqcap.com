Asset Location Optimization

Optimizing asset location is the most powerful tax lever most investors never pull.

Most focus on asset allocation — what to own, how much, and in what mix. Far fewer think carefully about where each investment actually lives. But placement matters just as much as selection. A REIT in a taxable account generates ordinary income taxed at your highest rate. The same REIT in a tax-deferred account compounds untouched for decades. The investment is identical. The outcome is not.

At STQ, asset location is the foundation of every portfolio we build — because getting it right can add hundreds of thousands of dollars over a lifetime without changing a single holding.

03

Synthetic Portfolio Borrowing

Need liquidity without selling? Box spreads offer a compelling path. By selling a short box spread on index options, you effectively borrow at near-Treasury rates — with no credit check, no bank approval, and no forced selling of appreciated positions. The interest is potentially tax-deductible, and you stay fully invested while accessing the capital you need.

At STQ, we implement synthetic borrowing through established platforms on the Schwab marketplace — giving you access to a liquidity tool that is both sophisticated and practical.

Direct Indexing

Direct indexing means owning the individual securities that make up an index — rather than a fund that holds them for you. The result is a portfolio that tracks your target exposure while giving you the ability to harvest losses at the individual security level, customize holdings around existing positions, and exclude specific sectors or companies.

For high-income investors in California, direct indexing can generate significantly more tax alpha than a comparable ETF strategy — because you're not waiting for the index to lose value. Individual stocks move independently, creating harvesting opportunities even in up markets.

At STQ, we implement direct indexing through institutional-grade separately managed accounts, giving you the benefits of index-like diversification with the tax efficiency of individual ownership.

Tax Loss Harvesting

Tax-loss harvesting turns market volatility into a tax advantage — strategically realizing losses to offset capital gains and reduce your tax bill, without disrupting your long-term investment strategy.

At STQ, we use advanced techniques — including long-short strategies — that continuously generate losses regardless of market direction. This creates a growing loss bank that can offset gains from real estate sales, business exits, concentrated stock positions, and more.

Harvested losses never expire. The earlier you start building the bank, the more powerful the tool becomes.

06

Concentrated Position Management

A concentrated position — whether from company stock, RSUs, a business sale, or an inheritance — is both an asset and a risk. Managing it requires balancing your desire to preserve upside, your need to diversify, and the tax cost of doing either.

At STQ, we build tailored strategies for each client's situation. Options overlays can create downside protection without a taxable sale. Systematic diversification over time can spread the tax impact across years. Tax-loss harvesting elsewhere in the portfolio can offset gains realized on the position. And in the right circumstances, charitable vehicles can eliminate the tax burden entirely.

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to a concentrated position. There is only the right answer for you.

Derivative-Based Strategies

Options aren't just for speculation. In the right hands, they're precision instruments for managing risk, generating income, and adjusting portfolio exposures without triggering taxable sales.

At STQ, we use options overlays to protect against significant downside, generate yield on existing positions, and reshape return profiles for clients with concentrated stock or large unrealized gains. Every structure is evaluated through a tax lens — because a hedge that triggers a taxable event may not be worth implementing.

Strategies include protective put spreads, cash-secured put writing, and covered calls — each deployed thoughtfully within your broader portfolio context.

Alternative Investments

Alternatives — private equity, private credit, venture, real estate, hedge funds — can be powerful portfolio diversifiers. But they are often highly tax-inefficient when placed incorrectly.

This is asset location in action. A fund generating short-term gains and ordinary income belongs in a tax-deferred account. A venture fund with long-term capital gain potential may belong in a taxable account where rates are lower. Getting this wrong can erase a significant portion of the return.

At STQ, every alternative is evaluated not just on its return potential, but on where its tax character is most favorable — so you keep more of what it earns.

Estate & Philanthropic Strategies

Wealth transfer is a tax problem as much as a planning problem. Without coordination, a lifetime of careful investing can be significantly eroded by estate taxes, income taxes on inherited assets, and inefficient gifting.

At STQ, we work in close collaboration with your attorney and CPA to implement strategies that preserve and transfer wealth efficiently — donor-advised funds for charitable giving, step-up in basis planning, trust structures, and asset location decisions made with the next generation in mind.

We don't replace your estate attorney. We make sure your investment strategy and your estate plan are actually talking to each other.

Wealth Transfer
11

Roth Conversion Strategies

A Roth conversion — moving assets from a traditional IRA or 401(k) into a Roth account — is one of the most powerful tax planning tools available to high earners, but timing and sizing matter enormously.

The goal is to convert in years when your marginal rate is lower than it will be in the future — filling up lower tax brackets deliberately, before required minimum distributions or Social Security income push you into a higher one. Done right, a Roth conversion strategy can shift millions of dollars into a permanently tax-free environment.

At STQ, we model conversion scenarios across your full financial picture — current income, future RMDs, estate objectives, and California-specific considerations — to identify the optimal conversion path for your situation.

Tax Planning
12

Tax-Smart Rebalancing

Every portfolio drifts. As markets move, your allocation shifts away from target — and restoring it requires trading. But trading has a tax cost, and that cost is often ignored.

At STQ, every rebalancing decision is evaluated through a tax lens first. We use new contributions to rebalance where possible, avoiding sales entirely. When we do sell, we harvest losses alongside gains to minimize net tax impact. And we sequence trades across accounts to take advantage of the different tax treatment each one offers.

Rebalancing is inevitable. The tax cost of doing it poorly is not.
